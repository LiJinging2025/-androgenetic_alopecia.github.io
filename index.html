Research Hotspots and Trends Analysis of Androgenetic Alopecia

An Agent-Autotuned Topic Modeling Approach with DeepSeek

Research Overview

This study addresses the massive and complex literature data in the field of Androgenetic Alopecia (AGA) by developing a deep learning-based agent-autotuned topic modeling approach. By combining natural language processing techniques, unsupervised clustering algorithms, and large language model feedback mechanisms, we achieve automatic topic discovery, hotspot identification, and trend analysis of AGA research literature.

Core Methodology: BERT Sentence Embeddings → UMAP Dimensionality Reduction → DBSCAN Clustering → c-TF-IDF Topic Extraction → DeepSeek Agent Tuning → Topic Model Optimization

The primary objective of this research is to provide clinicians, researchers, and patients with an intuitive, interactive knowledge graph of AGA research, facilitating rapid understanding of the current research landscape, development trends, and potential research directions in this field.

Detailed Implementation:

  • Data Collection: Compiled research literature on AGA from the past decade, creating a structured database with titles, abstracts, keywords, and publication years.
  • Text Processing: Implemented advanced NLP preprocessing including tokenization, stopword removal, and text normalization.
  • Embedding Generation: Utilized BERT-based models to create high-quality sentence embeddings that capture semantic meaning.
  • Visualization Pipeline: Developed a comprehensive visualization system that transforms high-dimensional embeddings into 2D/3D representations for intuitive analysis.
  • Agent Integration: Incorporated DeepSeek as an intelligent agent for parameter tuning and content optimization, creating a hybrid "unsupervised + supervised" methodology.
